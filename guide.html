John15Five · A Bible app

The Bible is the authority.
The app is the trellis.

A quiet, private place to spend time in God's Word — and what to expect if you're helping test it.

John15Five is named for John 15:5 — “abide in Me, and you bear much fruit.” The Bible is the point; the plans, journal, and study help are a trellis built to support your time in the Word, never to become the vine.

The whole Bible lives on your phone and works with no signal. Everything you write — journal, prayers, highlights — stays encrypted on your device. There's no account, no ads, and nothing about your reading is tracked.

What makes it different

You probably already have a Bible app. Most of them are built to hold your attention — streaks, notifications, an account, and your data. John15Five is built the opposite way: it points you to the Word and gets out of the way.

Truly private. The whole Bible and everything you write live on your phone, encrypted. No account, no ads, and we cannot see what you read.
Offline & free, always. Reading, journaling, and prayer work with no connection, and Scripture is never behind a paywall.
AI that can't rewrite Scripture. The study help points to real verses the app shows from the Bible on your phone — it never writes or paraphrases Scripture, and never rules on what faithful Christians disagree about.
Made for your life. Describe what you're walking through and it builds a reading plan — or photograph a printed plan from church and follow it. Keep several plans going at once and switch between them.
The whole Bible, narrated. Tap Listen on any chapter and hear it read aloud in a warm, natural voice — the entire Bible, not a robotic phone voice. It caches for offline listening, and you can download whole sets ahead of time.
Made to share — on your terms. Send a reading plan to a friend or your whole church group for them to follow, and share a day's reflection to a group chat, Messages, email, or social — but only ever when you choose to. Nothing is shared automatically.
Speak, and keep only your words. Journal and pray by voice; the recording is never saved, only the text you see.
Grace, not guilt. No streaks to break, no scores, no shame for a missed day — just a door left open.

Reading Scripture

Read the Bible

The whole Bible is on your phone and needs no connection, ever. Reading it leaves no record anywhere but your own device.

  • Open the Read tab, choose a book — start typing to jump to it — then a chapter.
  • Tap once to hide the bars and let it be just the text; tap again to bring them back.
  • Press and hold a verse to highlight it, mark it a favorite, add a note, or add it to a group.
  • The words of Jesus are shown in red.
  • It keeps your place, and marks a book as read once you've finished all of it.

Make it your own

A few quiet settings make the reading comfortable.

  • Translation: the Berean Standard Bible (the default), plus the World English Bible and the King James Version — all on your phone, all free.
  • Text size, and a Paragraph layout that reads like a book or a Verse-by-verse layout for study.
  • Light, Dark, or match your phone.

Search

Find a word or phrase, or jump straight to a reference.

  • Open Search from the reader or from Journey; results appear as you type.
  • Type a reference like “John 3:16” to go straight there.
  • Sort by best match, or in Bible order.

Related passages

See where Scripture speaks to Scripture. Press and hold a verse and tap “Related passages” for a list of cross-references, drawn from a large, human-voted open project. It works offline, with no AI.

Listen to a passage

Rather hear the Word than read it? Tap Listen at the top of the reader and the passage is read aloud in a warm, natural narrated voice. On a plan day it reads that day's reading; otherwise it reads the chapter you're in.

  • The narration covers the whole Bible. A chapter downloads the first time you play it, then plays from your phone — so after that first listen it works with no connection.
  • Tap “Stop reading” whenever you like; it also stops when you move to another chapter.
  • Heading somewhere with no signal? Download a set ahead of time under Settings → Listen offline, and reclaim the space there whenever you like.
  • On a chapter you haven't downloaded yet, with no connection, Listen falls back to your phone's own built-in voice — so it always works.

Marks and groups

Everything you mark is gathered under Journey → Marks. You can also collect verses into named groups you return to — “Comfort,” “Promises,” a sermon.

  • Highlight a verse in one of five colors; press and hold again to change or remove it.
  • Press and hold a verse, then “Add to a group” — name a new one on the spot.
  • Tag a mark to find it again later, and filter Marks by highlights, notes, or favorites.
  • Open a group from Journey → Collections; press and hold a verse to take it back out.

Reading plans & your daily rhythm

Reading plans

A plan gives you a passage a day with room to reflect. Four ways to start one, all under Journey → Plans.

  • Choose a ready-made plan from the library.
  • ✦ Build one for what you're walking through — describe your situation and the study companion writes a plan you review before it's yours. It takes a minute or so as it reads through Scripture, so keep the screen open while it builds.
  • ✦ Photograph a printed plan — snap the pages of a church handout and it becomes a plan you can follow.
  • Open a plan file someone shared with you.
  • Follow several at once and switch between them; set a plan down for later, delete one, or share a plan — the passages only, never your writing.

Your day

The Today tab shows one thing to do next — your plan's reading for the day, or the chapter where you left off. A ring of seven dots shows the past week at a glance, and a verse greets you once a day. No streaks, no numbers, no pressure.

Your daily rhythm

On a plan day: read, then reflect, journal, and pray — as much or as little as you like. Nothing is required, and nothing happens unless you tap.

  • Read today's passage, then mark the day read when you're ready.
  • A missed day is never a broken streak. The plan simply waits for you; there's no catching up.
  • Finish the whole plan and you get one quiet celebration — the only one in the app.

Reflecting: journal, prayer & study

Journaling

Write what a passage stirs up. Type it, or just say it out loud — there's a microphone on every writing box.

  • The day's reading sits right above the box so you can glance back at it.
  • Come back to the same day and you continue the same entry; it doesn't start over.
  • ✦ “Clean this up” adds punctuation to a spoken entry; “Give me back my own words” restores it exactly as you said it. Your own words are always kept.
  • Share a day's entry to a group, Messages, or email — only when you tap Share.

Prayer

Keep the things you're praying for, and mark how they're answered. Say them aloud if that's easier; prayer is a spoken thing.

  • Give a prayer a short title, then say or type it.
  • Later, add an update, mark it answered with a note, or gently set it down — and reopen it if you're praying again.

✦ Questions to sit with

A few open questions to send you back into the passage — never a quiz, and there's never a right answer.

  • Answer the ones you want, by voice or typing.
  • “Add to my journal” saves each answer as its own entry, labeled with the question.

✦ Understand a passage

Ask about the background and context of what you're reading. It opens with a short commentary and lets you ask more.

  • It stays close to Scripture and points you back to it — and to your pastor.
  • It never renders a verdict on matters faithful Christians disagree about.

Keeping it, and staying private

Your Journey

A record of what you've read, written, and prayed, newest first. No streaks, no scores, no percentages. It fills in as you go — a first reading, a book finished, a plan begun or completed, a prayer answered.

Keep it, print it

Turn your reading and reflections into a clean document you can hold.

  • On a plan: “Save this study as a PDF” — cover, passages, your journal and prayers.
  • On the Journal screen: “Save my journal as a PDF” — everything you've written, by day.
  • Print either one for a binder to keep and reread.

Speak instead of type

Every place you can write has a microphone. Your voice is transcribed on your phone — the audio is never uploaded and never even saved. Only the words you see remain.

Reminders

A gentle daily nudge, if you want one, under Settings → Daily reminder. It only ever invites — it never counts a thing against you.

Your privacy, plainly

Everything you write stays on your phone, encrypted, and there is no account. Reading the text is completely private — it never touches a server. A few optional features do reach one, only the moment you use them: building a plan, asking about a passage, cleaning up a spoken entry, photographing a printed plan, and — when you tap Listen — downloading that chapter's narration (a plain audio file, tied to no account). Nothing else ever leaves your phone.
