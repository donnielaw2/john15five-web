Privacy Policy

How John15Five handles your information — and why, in most cases, it never handles it at all.

Effective: July 15, 2026 Last updated: July 15, 2026 Publisher: FirstLight Technologies LLC

John15Five is a private, local-first Bible app. The Bible lives on your device and works with no connection. What you write — your journal, prayers, highlights, notes, and reading progress — is encrypted on your phone and does not leave it. There is no account, and nothing to sign up for.

This policy explains the few, deliberate cases where information does leave your device — always because you chose an action that requires it — and the many things we intentionally never collect.

1Who we are

John15Five (“the app,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) is published by FirstLight Technologies LLC. This policy applies to the John15Five mobile application on Apple iOS, including builds distributed for testing through Apple TestFlight.

2Our approach to your privacy

The app is built around a simple principle: your reading of Scripture and everything you write in response to it is between you and God — not something to be measured, stored on our servers, or sold. Wherever we had a choice between collecting information and not collecting it, we chose not to.

  • No account is required. You do not create a login, and we do not ask for your name, email, or phone number to use the app.
  • No advertising and no trackers. The app contains no ads and no third-party advertising or analytics trackers.
  • No selling of data. We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information to anyone, for any purpose.

3Information stored only on your device

The following stay on your device and are not transmitted to us. They are held in an encrypted database on the phone:

  • Your Bible reading — the passages you open and your place in a book.
  • Highlights, notes, favorites, and verse groups you create.
  • Reading plans you start, build, or import, and your progress through them.
  • Journal entries, prayers, and reflections you write.
  • Your settings and preferences.
In plain terms

If you use the app entirely offline, no personal information leaves your phone at all. Deleting the app removes this data from your device.

4Information that leaves your device — only when you act

A small number of optional features need to send information off the device to work. These run only when you choose to use them.

a. AI-assisted study and plan features

When you use an assisted feature — for example, asking the study companion to explain a passage, generating a reading plan from a situation you describe, or importing a plan by photographing a printed handout — the information required for that specific request is sent, over an encrypted connection, to our server and to our AI processing provider (Anthropic) to produce your result. Depending on the feature, this may include the text you typed, the passage reference on screen, or the photo you took.

  • These inputs are used only to generate your result. They are not used to build a profile of you, and there is no account to attach them to.
  • Your inputs are not used to train AI models.
  • The AI never returns Scripture text itself; it returns references, which the app resolves against the Bible already on your device.
  • Your journal entries and prayers are never sent to these features unless you explicitly choose to include that text in a request.

Anthropic processes these requests under its own privacy and data-usage terms and may retain inputs for a limited period for safety and abuse prevention. See Anthropic’s privacy policy at anthropic.com/legal/privacy.

b. Diagnostics and reliability

To keep the app stable, we may collect limited, anonymous technical information — such as crash reports, the app version, your device model, and the operating-system version. This is used only to find and fix problems. It never includes the content of your reading, journal, notes, or prayers.

c. Purchases (if offered)

If optional premium features are offered in the future, purchases are handled entirely by Apple. We may receive your subscription status (whether a subscription is active) so the app can unlock features, but we do not receive or store your payment-card details.

5What we deliberately do not collect

  • No tracking of your Scripture engagement. We do not collect which passages you read, when, or how often — not individually, not aggregated, not anonymized.
  • No location data.
  • No contact lists, photos, or files, except a single photo you deliberately take to import a printed plan (Section 4a).
  • No advertising identifiers and no cross-app or cross-site tracking.

6Third-party services we rely on

We use a small number of trusted providers to operate the app. Each handles data under its own privacy policy:

  • Apple — distributes the app (App Store and TestFlight) and processes any purchases.
  • Microsoft Azure — hosts the backend service that relays requests for the assisted features in Section 4a.
  • Anthropic — provides the AI processing for the assisted features.

We do not share your personal content with any party except as strictly needed to deliver a feature you requested.

7How your information is protected

Personal content on your device is stored in an encrypted database. Information transmitted for the assisted features is sent over encrypted (HTTPS) connections. No method of storage or transmission is ever perfectly secure, but we design the app so that the most sensitive things you create simply never leave your phone in the first place.

8Data retention and deletion

Because your personal content lives on your device, you control it. You can delete individual entries, marks, and plans in the app, and deleting the app removes all of your on-device data. We do not keep copies of your journal, prayers, marks, or reading on our servers.

9Children’s privacy

John15Five is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. Because the app requires no account and collects no personal profile, it does not gather such information in the ordinary course of use.

10Your choices and rights

Since your data lives on your device and no account exists, you exercise your privacy choices directly: use the app offline to keep everything local, choose when (or whether) to use the assisted features, and delete any content — or the entire app — at any time. Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights under laws such as the GDPR or CCPA; because we hold essentially no personal data about you on our servers, most such requests are satisfied simply by the on-device controls described above. You may contact us with any question or request.

11Changes to this policy

If we change how the app handles information, we will update this policy and revise the “Last updated” date above. For material changes, we will make a reasonable effort to note them within the app.

12Contact us
